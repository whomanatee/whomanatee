HOW TO USE THIS LIBRARY

  • these books are meant to be used as a reference library that you can use to grow your thinking
  • use them more as encyclopedias of knowledge instead of books that you need to have totally read
  • no need to agree or disagree with the ideas in these books, instead learn what others have said so that we can use this knowledge to reach higher understanding, so there is no need to put down others and their ideas, nor is there a need to idolize them
  • using any one of these books is enough to get going and thinking
  • go at your own pace, sometimes reading one sentence is enough to keep the mind occupied for days
  • sometimes it's useful to think about the name of the book, as in why did the author reduce the book to that title
  • peruse the table of contents to get an idea of what the author is trying to express
  • peruse quotes that are sometimes put at the beginning of chapters
  • peruse appendices
  • peruse introductions
  • peruse first chapters
  • sometimes it makes sense to skip ahead and peruse the last chapter
  • peruse any other chapters that catch your eye
  • some books also put summaries at the end of chapters, which are useful even before reading the chapter
  • Google Books sometimes has previews of books that have enough content to begin pondering the topics covered in the book without having a copy of it
